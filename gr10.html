COURSE INFO

COURSE DESCRIPTION

This course is designed to challenge students to push their abilities and to practice project-based autonomous learning. We will look at key theories in design and innovation in the 21st century while being introduced to all the tools and machines in the LCC Fab Lab. Through a mix of practical and theoretical exercises, students will be encouraged to learn how to think outside-the-box and to develop a sense of commitment and determination required to make innovative products and ideas.

COURSE STRUCTURE

The course structure is broken down into weekly & bi-weekly assignments, quizes on design theory and safety, and a year-long final project. Each week consists of an assignment lecture that introduces students to the ideas, technologies, and processes related to that assignment followed by lab time. All assignments should lead towards their final project. Each term will focus on different element of technical learning.

---> TERM 1 - introduction to all the tools, machines, and processes available in the LCC Fab Lab.
---> TERM 2 - focus on advanced learning in 3 core technical skills relating to final project goals.
---> TERM 3 - final project development.
---> ONGOING - design principles and practice.

STUDENT WEBSITES

Each student is required to maintain a personal website portfolio that clearly documents and reflects on student progress and the completion of the required assignments alongside key learning outcomes during the design process. Student websites should be broken into 3 sections: about / assignments / final project. Each entry should include the following:

---> Sketches & notes.

---> Clean images of work in progress.

---> Observation & reflections on the design process

---> Clear descriptions of the steps and technology used.

EVALUATION

Students will be evaluated on their overall effort and commitment in learning the design process. They will be assessed based off of the documentation and reflection posted on their websites that clearly demonstrates the progress and learning outcomes of completing their assignments and final project prototypes. Skills will be tracked and students will be given opportunities to acquire certified digital badges that demonstrate valuable accomplishments.

ASSIGNMENTS

Each week a new process is introduced along with an assignment. Each assignment assignment introduces students to the creative and technical potential of a particular tool, machine, or process. Students will need to demonstrate their ability to learn how to operate the machine by completing a specific task while also documenting how it works and testing tolerances. Each assignment should be seen as an opportunity to work towards a final project.

FINAL PROJECTS

Projects must follow the following criteria:

---> Solve a particular problem.

---> Consist of THREE Fab Lab Processes.

---> Incorporate elements related to the theme of circular Economy.


IMPORTANT NOTES: The following types of projects will not be permitted:

---> Solar or portable chargers.

---> Phone or gaming accessories.

---> Objects that combust or are deemed dangerous or irresponsible.

---> Pop culture items (all designs must be original and solve a problem).

---> Luxury items.


Successful projects will likely include:

---> Maximum social impact.

---> Realistic in time and scope.

---> Relate to personal interests.

EXPECTATIONS

---> Students are expected to follow all safety guidelines and demonstrate acceptable behavior while working in the Fab Lab and Design Studio at all times.

---> Students should expect to visit the LCC Fab Lab on occasion outside of class time in order to complete prototypes.

---> Students are expected to present projects and learning outcomes at the Design and Innovation Fair.
